Why this matters, and why now. On 20 July the police came to a peaceful students' march with their name plates removed, cut the internet across central Delhi, and the courts have so far declined to even watch the footage. Posts are already being deleted and accounts locked. If the only copies live on phones and social media, this record can be erased.

This archive exists so that it cannot be. Upload as soon as you can. The original file from your camera roll, sent early, is worth far more than a forward found later: forwards strip the technical data courts rely on, and every day a clip stays only on a platform is another day it can be taken down or lost. Get it into the vault while it still exists.

Your submission is never made public. We will not post it on this site or anywhere else, in any form, not even blurred. It goes into a sealed, encrypted archive and is shown to no one but the legal team that carries these cases forward, if and when proceedings begin.

You stay anonymous. No account. No name. No email. Our systems are built to never log IP addresses, and we publish the code so you can verify that.

Highest safety: open this page in Tor Browser, which hides even your network location. If you are at personal risk, do that.

Send originals. The file straight from your phone's gallery, not a WhatsApp/Telegram forward (forwarding strips the technical data that makes footage court-grade).
