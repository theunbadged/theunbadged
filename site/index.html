They removed the badges.
We keep the record.

On 20 July 2026 — the opening day of Parliament's Monsoon Session — Delhi Police lathi-charged and tear-gassed the “Chalo Sansad” march over the NEET-UG paper leaks. Police's own figures put the injured at about 60 protesters and 118+ personnel; over a hundred people were detained, mobile internet was cut in central Delhi with no published suspension order, and photo reporting shows personnel deployed without name badges. This archive exists to preserve the evidence — in a form that survives takedowns and holds up in court.

A citizen evidence archive. Not a rumor mill. Not a verdict.

What this is

  • A secure evidence vault. Every original file is SHA-256-hashed the moment it arrives, then never altered. Metadata intact, chain of custody logged, stored redundantly — reserved for legal process.
  • A public record. A timeline of the day's events and, as verification proceeds, redacted evidence — faces of protesters and bystanders blurred, locations generalized.
  • A path to accountability. Evidence packages prepared for courts, the NHRC, and rights organizations — including the pattern of badge concealment itself, which is misconduct and points up the chain of command.

What this is not

  • Not facial recognition. We never extract faces and search them against databases — not even officers'. Identification rests only on markers visible in footage (badge numbers, name plates, unit and vehicle markings) and corroborated witness testimony.
  • Not a public trial. We document incidents; courts declare guilt. No individual is named as guilty here before adjudication.
  • Not unverified. Everything public carries a status label, and nothing is presented as established fact until it earns it.

How to read this archive

Claimed  Reported or submitted, not yet independently verified.

Corroborated  Supported by two or more independent sources.

Court-ready  Original preserved with intact chain of custody, verified, and packaged for legal proceedings.

If you were there

If you have photos or video from the crackdown, or from any day of the protest — even shaky, even partial — it matters. Originals matter most: the file straight off your phone, not a WhatsApp forward.
