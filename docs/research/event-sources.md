# Sources — CJP "Chalo Sansad" march and police lathi-charge, Delhi (20–21 July 2026)

Compiled 2026-07-21, ~19:50 IST, via live web search and fetches. All times IST.

## Headline date finding (read first)

**Every outlet that gives a date places the march and the lathi-charge on Monday,
20 July 2026** — the opening day of Parliament's Monsoon Session — not 21 July as
stated in the research brief. 21 July (today) is the aftermath: protesters
reclaiming Jantar Mantar (protest day 24), detention/FIR confirmations, the Delhi
HC order on Sonam Wangchuk, political reactions, a Congress dharna at the PM's
residence, and solidarity protests in other cities. Outlets published crackdown
coverage on 21 July (e.g., Newslaundry's photo essay), which likely explains the
date confusion. The timeline JSON spans 18–21 July accordingly.

A note on the organiser: outlets consistently name the organising group as the
**Cockroach Janta Party (CJP)**, founded by **Abhijeet Dipke** — "CJP" here is the
party, not Citizens for Justice and Peace.

## Contradictions and open points between outlets

1. **Date of the march**: all dated sources say Monday 20 July; the research brief
   said 21 July. Resolved in favour of 20 July (march) + 21 July (aftermath).
2. **Injury counts (protesters)**: ~60 (police figure via ThePrint; hospital
   sources via Wikipedia; Daily Pioneer) vs 50+ (PTI) vs "more than 100"
   (Newslaundry, citing RML emergency intake incl. 10 police). Not necessarily
   inconsistent (ER intake ≠ confirmed protester injuries), but report the spread.
3. **Injury counts (police)**: 118+ is consistently the police's own claim
   (ThePrint, Deccan Chronicle, Daily Pioneer, Newslaundry). One police-cited
   total says 160+ police-side; Outlook's day-24 piece says 170+ injured total
   both sides. No independent verification of the 118 figure exists.
4. **Detention counts**: ~70 (police figure; ThePrint, Daily Pioneer, Wikipedia)
   vs 100+ (PTI/Deccan Chronicle). Both far exceed the brief's "two dozen+".
5. **Who was detained**: police say detainees "were not students"; eyewitness
   accounts (aggregated on Wikipedia) say students and minors were bused away.
   Dipke's detention is itself contested — PTC and South First reported it; CJP
   later said he was not detained (ThePrint).
6. **Who escalated first**: police (via PTI): stone-pelting and barricade breaches
   preceded the lathi-charge. Protesters (via Al Jazeera, Daily Pioneer): march
   was peaceful when the lathi-charge began. ThePrint's timeline complicates the
   police version: it logs lathi use at 7:30 am and a lathi-charge at 10:04 am,
   with the heavy protester-side violence (bottles, Patel Chowk) logged at
   3:08 pm. Both versions carried, attributed.
7. **Early police denial vs later admission**: Delhi Police said around midday
   (IANS) that "no such incident has taken place", then released injury figures
   for the same day's action by 12:10 pm (ThePrint) / evening (PTI).
8. **Crowd size**: "close to 1,000" (South First lede) vs 10,000+ (Wikipedia) vs
   "tens of thousands" (Deccan Herald headline) vs ~500,000 (Harsh Mander claim
   via South First). Wildly divergent; no settled figure.
9. **Protest start date**: June 6 (Daily Pioneer) vs June 20 (PNI) vs ~June 28
   implied by "day 24 on July 21" (Outlook). Unresolved.
10. **Pellet-gun claim**: CJP alleged pellet guns were used (Outlook search
    summary); no other outlet found reporting this. Claimed only, disputed by police.
