# 0001 — No biometric facial recognition

**Status:** Accepted (2026-07-21). Non-negotiable; not open to revision.

## Context

The obvious-seeming feature for identifying unbadged officers is face
extraction + search against public/scraped databases.

## Decision

The platform will never perform biometric facial recognition or face-search,
in any tier, for any purpose — including identifying officers.

Identification happens through:
1. Official markers visible in footage: badge number, name plate, rank
   insignia, unit/company markings, vehicle plate.
2. Corroborated witness testimony, always labeled as a claim pending
   corroboration.
3. Badge concealment logged as evidence in its own right — misconduct that
   pushes liability upward to command.

## Consequences

- Some individual officers will remain unidentified. Accountability for their
  acts routes upward via command responsibility (deployment rosters, duty
  logs — obtained by court order / RTI).
- The archive stays legally defensible, avoids misidentification harm, and
  denies the state the "vigilante mob" narrative.
- Actor-continuity linking across clips (same actor tracked by appearance and
  position across footage of one incident) is permitted — it is human
  editorial analysis, not biometric database search.
